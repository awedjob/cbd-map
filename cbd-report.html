The first sign of a dollar-terms recovery appeared in 2026-Q2, when the median rose from $67 to $80 on 62 sales. That sample is thin, so treat the uptick as early evidence rather than a trend.

5. The "Never Sold" Parcels (3,364 — 50% of the region)

Nearly half the quadrant has no sale transaction on any venue, ever. This is verified against OpenSea, the DCL Marketplace, and raw Ethereum receipts, which catch even off-market deals. Their history is simple. The Decentraland Foundation minted them to Vegas City in 2018. Vegas City held them inside estates for years. During the liquidation they were at most shuffled between Vegas City wallets, with no payment ever changing hands.

Why they exist: Vegas City received the district wholesale and monetized it through the casino business, not resale — so no market history accumulated. The liquidation has only processed roughly half the inventory. Implications: (1) a never-sold parcel has no price anchor at all — its fair value is simply the zone in §1; (2) the large estate-locked portion among them (~3,263 parcels sit in estates) can only move as whole estates; (3) any never-sold parcel suddenly getting a first "sale" at a high price deserves immediate suspicion — there is no organic history to justify it.
